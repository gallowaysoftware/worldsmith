A fog-of-war editor audited this prose against the author's sealed notebook and this
installment's licensed reveals. Anything it flags as a LEAK states a secret the reader

resolve a leak by cutting the scene's charge; resolve it by un-naming the secret and
leaving the pressure.

**No leak is exempt — least of all the climax.** A flagged sentence is not spared
because it is the dramatic high point or the line the scene was building toward; the big
reveal is exactly where leaks hide. If a LEAK quotes a Fix, apply that Fix. The most
common leak-fix is **scope**: a reveal licensed as a character's *personal* realization
has been extrapolated to its *strategic scale* — armies, cohorts, how many others, which
enemy worlds, the war it wins. Cut the scaled clause; keep the personal violation.
("…breed them into armies of navigators who would lead the fleets to Pharos, to Kepler,
to Brahe" → "…breed them into children who would wear her face.") The horror stays; only
its forbidden scope leaves.

# How to apply it

- Make the SPECIFIC change each note calls for: cut the slop word/line, convert a
  "not X, but Y" to a plain declarative, delete the over-explanation / narrated
  subtext, strip the personified fog or object-with-agency, fix the off-voice
  dialogue, land a limp ending on a concrete sensory image instead of stating the
  withheld outright.
- **Rule-breaks, continuity slips, and fog-of-war leaks are non-negotiable** — they
  must be gone from your output. For a leak, un-name the secret while keeping the
  scene's pressure; do not delete the tension along with the statement.
- **Surgical means surgical.** Keep every sentence the diagnosis did NOT flag,
  verbatim. Preserve paragraph structure and scene breaks. This is fix-only: do
  NOT expand, do NOT compress, do NOT change what happens.
- **Fix in place — rewrite, never delete.** Every flagged problem is repaired by
  REWRITING the offending span where it stands, not by removing it. A slop word is
  swapped, not the sentence dropped; a "not X but Y" becomes a declarative of the same
  weight; a leak is rephrased to un-name the secret while keeping the same number of
  beats on the page; anaphora is broken by varying the opener, NOT by cutting the line.
  Deleting flagged sentences silently shortens the installment and is a failure of this
  pass. **Your output must be within 3% of the input's word count.** If you find yourself
  removing material, you are doing the wrong job — put it back, reworded.
- **Never invent** to patch a hole. If cutting a flagged line leaves a small gap,
  close it with sensory or character texture already implied by the scene — never
  with a new world-fact, character, place, or event.
- Honour the world bible's rules and the fog-of-war: do not reveal anything the
  prose was keeping withheld.

# Output

The full corrected prose — same structure, same length, same events — with every
diagnosed problem resolved and nothing else altered. No notes, no preamble. First
byte: the first word of the opening sentence.
